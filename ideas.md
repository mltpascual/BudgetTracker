## Idea 1: "Sawali Weave" — Filipino Craft Minimalism

**Design Movement**: Filipino Vernacular Craft meets Digital Minimalism. Inspired by the woven bamboo (sawali) patterns and the earthy tones of rural Philippine landscapes.

**Core Principles**: (1) Organic textures that reference Filipino craft materials — subtle woven patterns, natural grain. (2) Generous breathing room — every element floats in calm negative space. (3) Warm neutrals grounded by a single vivid green accent. (4) The kalabaw mascot appears as a "guide" character, not a decoration.

**Color Philosophy**: The mint/sage green represents rice paddies and growth. White cards float like clouds over the green wash. The deep forest green text anchors everything to the earth. Green is dominant (80%), white is structural (15%), accent pops are rare (5%).

**Layout Paradigm**: Stacked card columns with asymmetric spacing. Cards have varying widths and slight overlap on desktop. On mobile, a single-column flow with generous vertical rhythm. The bottom nav bar uses pill-shaped active indicators.

**Signature Elements**: (1) A subtle woven/crosshatch texture overlay on the background. (2) The kalabaw mascot peeking from behind cards with speech bubbles containing budget tips.

**Interaction Philosophy**: Interactions feel like gentle nudges — soft haptic-like feedback through scale transforms on tap. The calculator numpad has satisfying press states with subtle depth changes.

**Animation**: Page transitions use a gentle vertical slide (200ms ease-out). Cards fade in with a staggered delay (50ms per card). The mascot has a subtle idle breathing animation. No bouncing, no spinning.

**Typography System**: Nunito (700, 800) for display headings — rounded, friendly, approachable. System UI / Inter for body text and data — clean, legible at small sizes. Numbers use tabular figures for alignment in financial data.

## Idea 2: "Jeepney Pop" — Bold Filipino Street Art

**Design Movement**: Philippine Street Art Pop meets Flat Design. Inspired by the colorful jeepney art tradition — bold outlines, saturated colors, playful typography.

**Core Principles**: (1) Bold, confident color blocks with hard edges. (2) Playful but functional — every decorative element serves navigation or hierarchy. (3) The kalabaw mascot is expressive and animated, appearing in multiple poses. (4) Typography is loud and proud.

**Color Philosophy**: Emerald green as the hero color, but used in bold blocks rather than washes. Bright white creates stark contrast. Pops of coral/orange for warnings and debt. The palette is 60% green, 25% white, 15% accent colors.

**Layout Paradigm**: Grid-breaking card layouts with bold borders instead of shadows. Cards use thick 2px green borders. Some cards are full-bleed, others are inset. The bottom nav uses bold icon outlines with text labels.

**Signature Elements**: (1) Thick decorative borders on cards reminiscent of jeepney panel art. (2) The kalabaw mascot in a jeepney-driver hat, with exaggerated expressions.

**Interaction Philosophy**: Taps produce bold, snappy responses — quick scale-down-up (100ms). Swipe gestures for deleting transactions. The numpad keys have a satisfying "click" visual feedback with color inversion.

**Animation**: Elements enter with a confident slide-in from the left (150ms). Delete animations use a satisfying swipe-away. Page transitions are instant cuts with a subtle fade. Mascot reacts to user actions (happy when saving, worried when overspending).

**Typography System**: Nunito Black (900) for headlines — maximum friendliness and impact. Nunito Regular (400-600) for body — consistent family, clean hierarchy. All caps for section labels with generous letter-spacing.

## Idea 3: "Bahay Kubo" — Warm Organic Sanctuary

**Design Movement**: Organic Sanctuary Design. Inspired by the warmth and simplicity of a bahay kubo (nipa hut) — natural materials, soft light filtering through bamboo, a sense of home and safety.

**Core Principles**: (1) Everything feels soft and safe — no hard edges, no harsh contrasts. (2) The green palette leans warm (sage, olive) rather than cool (mint, teal). (3) Depth comes from layered translucency, not shadows. (4) The kalabaw is a cozy companion, not a mascot — it lives in the app like a pet.

**Color Philosophy**: Warm sage green as the primary wash — like sunlight through leaves. Cards use frosted glass (backdrop-blur) for a dreamy layered feel. Text is warm charcoal rather than pure black. The palette evokes a lazy afternoon in the province.

**Layout Paradigm**: Flowing single-column layout with rounded "bubble" cards that feel like they're floating. No hard grid lines. Cards have generous internal padding and rounded corners (24px+). The bottom nav uses soft blob-shaped indicators.

**Signature Elements**: (1) Frosted glass cards with a very subtle green tint. (2) The kalabaw appears as a small companion in the corner of the home screen, sleeping when the app is idle, alert when there are pending items.

**Interaction Philosophy**: Everything moves slowly and gently — like a hammock swaying. Long-press to edit, gentle swipe to archive. The numpad has soft, pillowy button states.

**Animation**: Slow, organic transitions (300ms ease-in-out). Cards float in with a gentle rise (translateY from 20px). Background has a very subtle parallax drift. The mascot has slow, ambient animations (ear twitch, tail swish).

**Typography System**: Quicksand (600, 700) for display — ultra-rounded, soft, warm. Nunito (400, 500) for body — maintains the rounded aesthetic at smaller sizes. Numbers are slightly larger than surrounding text for easy scanning.

## Selected Approach: Idea 1 — "Sawali Weave" (Filipino Craft Minimalism)

This approach best matches the Tarsi reference site's calm, clean aesthetic while giving Tipid its own Filipino identity through the kalabaw mascot and subtle craft-inspired textures. It keeps the same mint/sage green palette, generous whitespace, and friendly typography that makes Tarsi feel approachable, while the woven texture overlay and mascot speech bubbles create a distinct visual identity.
